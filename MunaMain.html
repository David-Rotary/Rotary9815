The briefing

What is MUNA?

The Model United Nations Assembly is an inter-school debating event where a team of six students forms a delegation, is assigned a country, and debates that country's position on set resolutions in a simulated UN General Assembly.

Unlike other UN school programs, Rotary MUNA is about debating resolutions rather than drafting amendments. Delegates represent the viewpoint of their assigned member state, with the goal of promoting peace and mutual understanding — gaining real experience in lobbying, diplomacy and the voting process along the way.

Who it's for: students in Years 10 and 11 interested in how the UN General Assembly works. What it costs: nothing — a local Rotary club sponsors each delegation, so it's free for students to take part.

ℹ️ This is not a UNAA (Model UN in Schools) or Evatt (UN Youth Australia) event.

The essentials

A half-day simulation that approximates the UN General Assembly — organised by a committee of Rotarians.

  • 👥 Team of six students, divided into three pairs
  • 🗳️ Three resolutions on peace & security, the environment, and geopolitics
  • 🎓 Years 10 & 11 — a powerful extracurricular for the VCE
  • 💛 Free to students — sponsored by your local Rotary club
The new format

One nation. Six students. Three pairs.

Your school sends a delegation of six, split into three pairs. Each pair takes the floor for one resolution — so everyone debates.

The speaking order & timing

Each resolution has a Sponsor and a Seconder, with speakers alternating 'for' and 'against' under set time limits.

  • Sponsor
    4 minutes
  • Seconder
    3 minutes
  • First Against
    4 minutes
  • All other countries
    3 minutes each
  • First Against · Right of Reply
    4 minutes
  • Sponsor · Right of Reply
    3 minutes

Get prepared

Your pre-work checklist.

Your country has a position on every resolution — your job is to find it, then defend it. (Remember: Resolution 3 is unprepared, so practise thinking on your feet.)

  • ✓Sort your team into three pairsSix students, one pair per resolution — decide who debates what.
  • ✓Research your allocated countryGovernment, history, and relationships with other UN nations.
  • ✓Read the two prepared resolutionsMigration & Development, and Weapons in Outer Space — decide for or against.
  • ✓Check the UN voting recordHow has your country actually voted on similar issues?
  • ✓Decide sponsor / seconder rolesWill your delegation sponsor or second a resolution? Speak first against?
  • ✓Practise speaking to time3–4 minute speeches, no devices — rehearse the unprepared format too.
  • ✓Plan your national costume & flagRespectful, culturally appropriate dress; Pair 1 carries the flag in the parade.
