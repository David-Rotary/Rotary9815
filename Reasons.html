Should I go to District Conference?

A weekend away. The cost. The drive home on Sunday night. We understand the reasons to stay home — so let's be honest about them first, then talk about why so many Rotarians are glad they came anyway.

The reasons you might stay home

Every one of these is real, and most of us have felt at least a few of them.

1The cost — registration, accommodation and meals add up, especially for a couple.
2The travel, particularly from the further-flung corners of the district.
3Giving up a precious weekend.
4Family or carer commitments that don't pause.
5Work or business demands that don't stop for Saturday.
6A feeling that "it's not really for me" — that conference is for district leaders.
7Being newer, and unsure you'll know anyone there.
8Hesitation about turning up on your own.
9A sense that the programme is the same every year.
10A past conference that felt long, dry or over-formal.
11No clear answer to "what will I actually get out of it?"
12Assuming the highlights will turn up online or in a summary anyway.
13Health or mobility concerns.
14A clash with a club, community or personal event.
15Simply not feeling connected enough to the wider district to make the effort.
And yet…

Ask anyone who went last year. The reasons to stay home are real — but they're rarely the ones people remember by Sunday afternoon.

The reasons you'll be glad you went

This is what a weekend together actually looks like.

1
FellowshipReconnecting with old friends and meeting Rotarians from right across the district.
2
InspirationSpeakers and stories that reignite exactly why you joined Rotary.
3
LearningSessions and workshops that grow you, personally and as a Rotarian.
4
Fresh ideasService and project ideas you can carry straight back to your own club.
5
RecognitionCelebrating the achievements of clubs and members — maybe your own.
6
The bigger pictureSeeing Rotary beyond your club, and where you fit within it.
7
Meet the leadershipTime with the District Governor and district team, in person.
8
NetworkingConversations that spark inter-club partnerships and joint projects.
9
Club prideRepresenting your club and flying its flag among peers.
10
MentorshipNewer members learning from old hands — and the other way around.
11
Global reachA real sense of Rotary's worldwide impact and The Rotary Foundation's work.
12
A great weekendSocial events and entertainment that are simply good fun.
13
A change of sceneTime away, often somewhere well worth visiting.
14
BelongingA stronger sense of being part of something larger than your own club.
15
A voiceUnderstanding — and helping shape — the direction of the district.

Your District Conference. Be part of it.

Come for one reason. You'll go home with fifteen.
